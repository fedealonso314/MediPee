# MediPee
“Measurement of urine pH in healthy individuals aged 13 to 30 years in the capital of Paraguay in order to establish a relationship  between urine pH and overweight.”

Urine pH is one of the various parameters obtained during a complete body check-up and reflects both metabolic activity and overall well-being. In a healthy person, urine pH is typically around 6 (however, it can range from 4.5 to 8), making it slightly acidic. Diet actively influences urinary pH, increasing it with strictly vegetarian diets and decreasing it with more meat-based or protein-rich diets. However, there are certain situations in which a change in urinary pH can indicate that something is wrong in the body. A value that is too high or too low can contribute to the formation of kidney stones (renal lithiasis), urinary tract infections, or other conditions related to the urinary tract and the acid-base balance of urine. Obesity is a global health problem whose prevalence has increased markedly in recent years, with serious socioeconomic consequences. Parallel to this increase in obesity prevalence, there has been a marked increase in the prevalence of diseases related to the acid-base balance of urine.
Beyond the coexistence of an increase in the incidence and prevalence of obesity and urinary lithiasis, large epidemiological studies have shown that the frequency of urinary lithiasis increases with increasing body weight and body mass index (BMI).
Based on the evidence described above, this project aims to determine the possible relationship between body weight and urinary pH, as well as between BMI and urinary pH.
